Liquid Glass Refraction

SVG feDisplacementMap applied via backdrop-filter — the effect that's taken over the web since Apple introduced it at WWDC 2025

True Optical Refraction

Unlike flat glassmorphism that just blurs the background, Liquid Glass physically bends the pixels behind it — simulating the optics of convex glass. The technique encodes Snell's Law of refraction into an SVG displacement map, then pipes it through backdrop-filter: url(#filter) to distort whatever sits behind the panel in real time.

🍎

iOS 26 Origin

Apple debuted Liquid Glass at WWDC 2025 across iOS 26, macOS Tahoe, and visionOS 3 — the web replicated it within weeks.

⚙️

SVG Filter Pipe

feTurbulence → feColorMatrix → feDisplacementMap creates a noise-based lens that bends pixels by up to 14px in each axis.

✨

Specular Rim

A CSS ::before pseudo with a directional gradient adds the specular highlight at the glass edge — no WebGL needed.

⚠️ Browser note: SVG filters as backdrop-filter currently require Chrome/Chromium. Firefox and Safari fall back to standard glassmorphism blur — the visual still works, just without pixel-bending.
