How it works

The W3C Scroll-Driven Animations spec landed in all major browsers in 2024–2025. It introduces animation-timeline with two axes: scroll() — tied to a container's scroll position — and view() — tied to when an element crosses the viewport boundary. Combine with animation-range to express exactly which part of the entry/exit phase each animation covers. No ResizeObserver, no getBoundingClientRect polling, no requestAnimationFrame — just declarative CSS that the compositor handles at 60 fps.
