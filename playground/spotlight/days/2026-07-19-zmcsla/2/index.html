Sibling Math

Two new CSS functions — sibling-index() and sibling-count() — let layouts compute their own position and spacing with zero JavaScript. Drag the item-count slider and every demo below recalculates itself instantly.

1 · Staggered cascade

Sequential entrance delay driven entirely by an element's position among its siblings.

animation-delay: calc(sibling-index() * 90ms);

2 · Self-dividing tabs

Each tab claims an equal share of the row — add or remove siblings and widths rebalance automatically.

width: calc(100% / sibling-count());

3 · Radial menu

Native CSS trigonometry spaces items evenly around a circle — a hexagon becomes a decagon with one more sibling.

--angle: calc((360deg / sibling-count()) * sibling-index());
