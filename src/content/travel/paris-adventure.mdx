Last month, I had the incredible opportunity to spend a week in Paris, capturing the essence of this magnificent city through my lens. From the iconic landmarks to hidden neighborhood gems, every corner told a story worth preserving.

## The Journey Begins

Landing at Charles de Gaulle, the excitement was palpable. Paris had been on my photography bucket list for years, and finally being there felt surreal. The plan was simple: wake up early, explore different neighborhoods each day, and capture both the famous sights and the everyday moments that make Paris unique.

## Day 1: Classic Landmarks

Starting with the classics seemed appropriate. The Eiffel Tower at sunrise, when the city is still quiet and the light is golden, provided some of the most breathtaking shots of the trip.

**Pro tip:** The best time to photograph the Eiffel Tower is between 6-7 AM when there are fewer crowds and the morning light creates beautiful silhouettes.

## Day 2-3: Neighborhood Wandering

### Montmartre Magic

Montmartre offered a completely different perspective of Paris. The cobblestone streets, artist studios, and the stunning view from Sacré-Cœur created a romantic atmosphere that's impossible to capture fully in words.

### Le Marais District

The historic Jewish quarter provided incredible architectural photography opportunities. The contrast between medieval buildings and modern boutiques created fascinating juxtapositions.

## Photography Techniques Used

Throughout the week, I experimented with various techniques:

- **Golden Hour Shooting** - Most photos were taken during the first and last hour of sunlight
- **Street Photography** - Capturing candid moments of Parisians going about their daily lives
- **Architectural Details** - Focusing on the intricate details that make French architecture unique
- **Long Exposure** - Using ND filters for silky water effects along the Seine

## The Food Scene

No Paris adventure is complete without diving into the culinary culture. From croissants at dawn to wine at dusk, every meal was a photographic opportunity.

### Must-Try Experiences:
1. **Morning pastries** at a local boulangerie
2. **Lunch at a bistro** in Saint-Germain
3. **Sunset wine** along the Seine
4. **Late dinner** in the Latin Quarter

## Equipment Notes

For this trip, I traveled light with just two lenses:
- **24-70mm f/2.8** - Perfect for street photography and general use
- **85mm f/1.4** - Ideal for portraits and isolating subjects

The decision to pack light paid off, allowing me to walk for hours without fatigue and be more spontaneous with my compositions.

## Final Thoughts

Paris exceeded every expectation. Beyond the photographs, the city offered countless moments of inspiration and wonder. The way light dances off the Haussmann buildings at sunset, the energy of the morning markets, and the quiet contemplation possible in the city's many parks – all of these experiences shaped not just my photography, but my perspective on travel itself.

This trip reminded me why I'm passionate about travel photography: it's not just about capturing beautiful images, but about preserving the feelings and stories that make each destination unique.

## Planning Your Own Paris Photo Adventure

If you're planning a photography trip to Paris, here are my top recommendations:

- **Stay in different arrondissements** to experience various neighborhoods
- **Wake up early** for the best light and fewer crowds
- **Bring comfortable walking shoes** - you'll be doing a lot of walking
- **Learn basic French phrases** - locals appreciate the effort
- **Don't over-plan** - some of the best shots come from spontaneous discoveries

Paris will always hold a special place in my heart and photography portfolio. Until next time, au revoir!
