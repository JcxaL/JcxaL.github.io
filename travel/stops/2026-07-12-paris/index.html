Paris

A week in the City of Light, ridden like a metro line — photography, food, and the everyday moments between the landmarks.

Jan 20248 min read 6 stationsField note
Golden hourStreet ArchitectureLong exposure
  1. 01

    Charles de Gaulle

    Arrivée
    Day 1 · Landing

    Landing at CDG, the excitement was palpable — Paris had sat on the bucket list for years. The plan was simple: wake early, explore a different neighborhood each day, and capture both the famous sights and the ordinary moments that make the city itself.

  2. 02

    Tour Eiffel

    Lever du soleil
    Day 1 · 06:30

    The Eiffel Tower at sunrise, when the city is still quiet and the light turns gold — some of the most breathtaking frames of the whole trip.

    Pro tip — shoot between 6–7 AM: fewer crowds, and the low morning light throws long, clean silhouettes.
  3. 03

    Montmartre

    Sacré-Cœur
    Day 2 · Morning

    A completely different Paris. Cobblestone streets, artist studios, and the sweeping view from Sacré-Cœur created a romance that's impossible to fully put into words — so I let the camera do the talking.

  4. 04

    Le Marais

    Le quartier historique
    Day 3 · Afternoon

    The historic Jewish quarter was an architectural playground. The contrast between medieval façades and modern boutiques made for endlessly fascinating juxtapositions — old stone against new glass.

  5. 05

    La Seine

    À table
    Days 4–6 · The food line

    No Paris trip is complete without eating your way through it: morning pastries at a neighborhood boulangerie, a long bistro lunch in Saint-Germain, sunset wine along the Seine, and late dinners in the Latin Quarter. Every meal was its own photograph.

  6. 06

    Terminus

    Notes de matériel
    The kit

    Packed light and it paid off: a 24–70mm f/2.8 for street and general use, an 85mm f/1.4 for portraits and isolating subjects. Two lenses meant hours of walking without fatigue — and far more spontaneity in the compositions.
